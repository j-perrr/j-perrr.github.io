The Amazon

5 FACTS ABOUT THE AMAZON

  1. With nine and a half million hectares, the Amazonian watershed of Peru covers 75% of the entire territory of Peru
  2. The Amazon Region produces most of the country's resources: oil, gold and hardwood
  3. the Amazon rainforest of Peru is among the the world's top 5 most biologically diverse areas
  4. There are only about 10 major ecosystems in the Amazon lowlands
  5. Peru belongs to the world's top 5 richest countries in numbers of birds and mammals

Biodiversity

Harpy Eagle, Muscovy Duck, Hoatzin, Scarlet Macaw, Common Potoo, Blue and Yellow Macaw, Chestnut-fronted Macaw, Amarican Darter all live in the Amazon region of Peru.

The Amazon stretches from the high Andes via the Andean foothills to the almost flat, tropical lowlands. It is in this region, and particularly the lowlands, where Peru's biodiversity is by far the richest.

Demographics

The tropical rainforest of the Amazon of Peru covers about 60% of of Peru, but just about of a tenth of the population live there.
